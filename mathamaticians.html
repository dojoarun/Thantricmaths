Johann Carl Friedrich Gauss was a German mathematician and physicist who made significant contributions to many fields in mathematics and sciences.
Born: 30 April 1777, Brunswick, Germany
Died: 23 February 1855, Göttingen, Germany
Education: University of Helmstedt, University of Göttingen, Technical University of Braunschweig
Awards: Copley Medal


Leonhard Euler was a Swiss mathematician, physicist, astronomer, logician and engineer who made important and influential discoveries in many branches of mathematics.
Born: 15 April 1707, Basel, Switzerland
Died: 18 September 1783, Saint Petersburg, Russia
Residence: Kingdom of Prussia; Russian Empire; Switzerland
Education: University of Basel (1720–1723), University of Basel, Saint Petersburg State University
Spouse: Salome Abigail Gsell (m. 1776–1783), Katharina Gsell (m. 1734–1773)


Srinivasa Ramanujan FRS was an Indian mathematician who lived during the British Rule in India. Though he had almost no formal training in pure mathematics, he made substantial contributions to mathematical analysis, number theory, infinite series, and continued fractions, including solutions to mathematical problems then considered to be unsolvable.
Born: 22 December 1887, Erode
Died: 26 April 1920, Kumbakonam
Award: Fellow of the Royal Society
Education: Trinity College (1919–1920), Government Arts College (no degree), Pachaiyappa's College (no degree)
Parents: Komalatammal, K. Srinivasa Iyengar


Euclid, sometimes called Euclid of Alexandria to distinguish him from Euclid of Megara, was a Greek mathematician, often referred to as the "founder of geometry" or the "father of geometry". He was active in Alexandria during the reign of Ptolemy I.
Nationality: Greek
Born: Mid-4th century BC
Known for: Euclidean geometry; Euclid's Elements; Euclidean algorithm
Died: Mid-3rd century BC


Alan Mathison Turing OBE FRS was an English mathematician, computer scientist, logician, cryptanalyst, philosopher and theoretical biologist.
Born: 23 June 1912, Maida Vale
Died: 7 June 1954, Wilmslow, United Kingdom
Awards: Smith's Prize (1936)
Partner(s): Joan Clarke; (engaged in 1941; did not marry)
Education: Princeton University (1936–1938)


Sir Isaac Newton FRS PRS was an English mathematician, physicist, astronomer, theologian, and author who is widely recognised as one of the most influential scientists of all time, and a key figure in the scientific revolution.
Born: 4 January 1643, Woolsthorpe Manor House, United Kingdom
Died: 31 March 1727, Kensington, London, United Kingdom
Full name: Sir Isaac Newton
Education: Trinity College (1667–1668)


Pythagoras of Samos was an ancient Ionian Greek philosopher and the eponymous founder of Pythagoreanism. His political and religious teachings were well known in Magna Graecia and influenced the philosophies of Plato, Aristotle, and, through them, Western philosophy.
Born: Samos, Greece
Died: Metapontum
Full name: Pythagoras of Samos
Books: The Order and the Doctrine of Pythagoras, The Golden Verses of Pythagoras: And Other Pythagorean Fragments
Nationality: Greek, Ancient Greek


Pierre de Fermat was a French lawyer at the Parlement of Toulouse, France, and a mathematician who is given credit for early developments that led to infinitesimal calculus, including his technique of adequality.
Born: 1607, Beaumont-de-Lomagne, France
Died: 12 January 1665, Castres, France
Education: University of Orléans (1623–1626)
Spouse: Louise Long Fermat
Books: Writings on Geometrical Loci
Parents: Françoise Cazeneuve Fermat, Dominique Fermat


Georg Friedrich Bernhard Riemann was a German mathematician who made contributions to analysis, number theory, and differential geometry. In the field of real analysis, he is mostly known for the first rigorous formulation of the integral, the Riemann integral, and his work on Fourier series.
Born: 17 September 1826, Kingdom of Hanover
Died: 20 July 1866, Verbania, Italy
Full name: Georg Friedrich Bernhard Riemann
Spouse: Elise Koch (m. 1862–1866)
Books: Collected Papers, On the Hypotheses Which Lie at the Bases of Geometry
Education: Humboldt University of Berlin, University of Göttingen


Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz was a prominent German polymath and philosopher in the history of mathematics and the history of philosophy. His most notable accomplishment was conceiving the ideas of differential and integral calculus, independently of Isaac Newton's contemporaneous developments.
Born: 1 July 1646, Leipzig, Germany
Died: 14 November 1716, Hanover, Germany
Influenced: Immanuel Kant, David Hume, Friedrich Nietzsche
Influenced by: René Descartes, Christiaan Huygens
Education: Leipzig University, University of Altdorf, University of Jena


Archimedes of Syracuse was a Greek mathematician, physicist, engineer, inventor, and astronomer. Although few details of his life are known, he is regarded as one of the leading scientists in classical antiquity.
Born: 288 BC, Syracuse, Italy
Died: 212 BC, Syracuse, Italy
Full name: Archimedes of Syracuse
Nationality: Greek
Parents: Phidias


David Hilbert was a German mathematician and one of the most influential and universal mathematicians of the 19th and early 20th centuries.
Born: 23 January 1862, Königsberg
Died: 14 February 1943, Göttingen, Germany
Spouse: Käthe Jerosch (m. 1892–1943)
Education: University of Königsberg (1880–1885)
Known for: Hilbert's basis theorem, Hilbert's axioms


Aryabhata I was the first of the major mathematician-astronomers from the classical age of Indian mathematics and Indian astronomy. His works include the Āryabhaṭīya and the Arya-siddhanta. For his explicit mention of the relativity of motion, he also qualifies as a major early physicist.
Born: 476 AD, Pataliputra
Died: 550 AD, India
Nationality: Indian
Era: Gupta Empire
Books: Aryabhatiya
Influenced: Lalla, Bhāskara I, Brahmagupta, Varāhamihira


John von Neumann was a Hungarian-American mathematician, physicist, computer scientist, and polymath.
Born: 28 December 1903, Budapest, Hungary
Died: 8 February 1957, Walter Reed National Military Medical Center, Bethesda
Education: Eötvös Loránd University, ETH Zürich, Fasori Gimnázium
Awards: Enrico Fermi Award, Bôcher Memorial Prize, Medal for Merit, Medal of Freedom

    Died: 31 May 1832, Paris, France

    Blaise Pascal was a French mathematician, physicist, inventor, writer and Catholic theologian. He was a child prodigy who was educated by his father, a tax collector in Rouen.
    Born: 19 June 1623, Clermont-Ferrand, France
    Died: 19 August 1662, Paris, France
    Nationality: French
    Notable ideas: Pascal's Wager; Pascal's triangle; Pascal's law; Pascal's theorem


    Fibonacci was an Italian mathematician from the Republic of Pisa, considered to be "the most talented Western mathematician of the Middle Ages". The name he is commonly called, Fibonacci, was made up in 1838 by the Franco-Italian historian Guillaume Libri and is short for filius Bonacci.
    Born: 1170, Pisa, Italy
    Full name: Leonardo Pisano Bigollo
    Nationality: Italian
    Date of death: 1240
    Parents: Guglielmo Bonacci, Alessandra Bonacci


    ules Henri Poincaré was a French mathematician, theoretical physicist, engineer, and philosopher of science. He is often described as a polymath, and in mathematics as "The Last Universalist," since he excelled in all fields of the discipline as it existed during his lifetime.
    Born: 29 April 1854, Nancy, France
    Died: 17 July 1912, Paris, France
    Education: Mines ParisTech, Lycée Louis-le-Grand, University of Paris, École Polytechnique
    Awards: Gold Medal of the Royal Astronomical Society, Bruce Medal, Matteucci Medal, Sylvester Medal, Bolyai Prize, Poncelet Prize

    Born: 28 April 1906, Austria-Hungary

    Education: University of Vienna
    Spouse: Adele Nimbursky Porkert (m. 1938–1978)
    Awards: Josiah Willard Gibbs Lectureship, Albert Einstein Award, National Medal of Science for Mathematics and Computer Science


    Claudius Ptolemy was a mathematician, astronomer, geographer and astrologer. He lived in the city of Alexandria in the Roman province of Egypt, under the rule of the Roman Empire, had a Latin name,which several historians have taken to imply he was also a Roman citizen, cited Greek philosophers, and used Babylonian observations and Babylonian lunar theory.
    Born: 100 AD, Alexandria, Egypt
    Died: Alexandria, Egypt
    Full name: Claudius Ptolemy
    Nationality: Roman

    Brahmagupta was an Indian mathematician and astronomer. He is the author of two early works on mathematics and astronomy: the Brāhmasphuṭasiddhānta, a theoretical treatise, and the Khaṇḍakhādyaka, a more practical text. Brahmagupta was the first to give rules to compute with zero.
    Born: 598 AD, Bhinmal
    Died: 668 AD, India
    Nationality: Indian
    Parents: Jishnugupta

    Born: 25 April 1849, Düsseldorf, Germany
    Died: 22 June 1925, Göttingen, Germany

    Born: 28 March 1928, Berlin, Germany
    Died: 13 November 2014, Saint-Girons, France

Nash's work has provided insight into the factors that govern chance and decision-making inside complex systems found in everyday life.

    Spouse: Alicia Nash (m. 2001–2015), Alicia Nash (m. 1957–1963)

    Died: 16 May 1830, Paris, France
    Education: École Normale Supérieure
    Known for: Fourier series; Fourier transform; Fourier's law of conduction; Fourier–Motzkin elimination
    Place of burial: Père Lachaise Cemetery, Paris, France
    Books: The Analytical Theory of Heat

    Died: 194 BC, Alexandria, Egypt
    Known for: Sieve of Eratosthenes Foundation of Geography

    Hipparchus of Nicaea was a Greek astronomer, geographer, and mathematician. He is considered the founder of trigonometry but is most famous for his incidental discovery of precession of the equinoxes. Hipparchus was born in Nicaea, Bithynia, and probably died on the island of Rhodes, Greece.
    Born: 190 BC, Nicaea
    Died: 120 BC, Rhodes, Greece
    Nationality: Greek
    Books: On Sizes and Distances, The geographical fragments of Hipparchus
